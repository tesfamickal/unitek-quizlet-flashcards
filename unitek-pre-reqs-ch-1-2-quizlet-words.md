## Terms and definitions

1. -algia — pain, suffering
2. dys- — bad, painful, difficult
3. -ectomy — surgical removal
4. Hyper- — excessive
5. Hypo- — deficient, decreased
6. -itis — inflammation
7. -osis — abnormal condition
8. -ostomy — surgically create an opening
9. -otomy — surgical incision
10. -plasty — surgical repair
11. -rrhage — bleeding, abnormal excessive fluid discharge
12. -rraphy — surgical suturing
13. -rrhea — flow, discharge
14. -rrhexis — rupture
15. -sclerosis — abnormal hardening
16. necrosis — tissue death
17. -malacia — abnormal softening
18. myel/o — spinal cord, bone marrow
19. oste/o — bone
20. neur/o — Nerve
21. Arthr/o — joint
22. cyan/o — blue
23. erythr/o — red
24. leuk/o — White
25. Melan/o — Black
26. poli/o — gray
27. tonsill/o — tonsils
28. crani/o — skull
29. arteri/o, arter/o — word root for artery
30. -megaly — enlargement
31. -dynia — pain
32. costal — ribs
33. sub — under, below
34. supra- — above, upper
35. abdominal cavity — contains primarily the major organs of digestion
36. -scropy — visual examination
37. my/o — muscle
38. -graphy — the process of producing a picture or record
39. hem/o — blood
40. ab — away from
41. dextr/o — right side
42. Ex- — out, away from
43. Endo- — within, inner
44. inter — between
45. intra — within
46. sinistr/o — left side
47. mega-, megalo- — large, great
48. olig/o — scanty, few
49. ad — toward
50. Eu- — good, normal
51. sign — objective evidence of disease such as a fever
52. Different diagnosis — The process of differentiating Between two or more conditions that similar signs or symptoms
53. eponym — a disease, structure, operation, or procedure named for the person who discovered or described it first
54. syndrome — a group of symptoms
55. acute — A condition with a rapid onset with a short course.
56. remission — the temporary, partial, or complete disappearance of the symptoms of a disease without having achieved a cure
57. prognosis — a prediction of the course of a disease
58. pyrosis — heartburn
59. fissure — an opening; a groove; a split
60. Fisula — abnormal passageway between two organs or between an internal organ and the body surface
61. ileum — the last and longest portion of the small intestine
62. ilium — part of the hip bone
63. Meningitis — inflammation and infection of the meninges
64. laceration — jagged wound
65. malaise — a feeling of depression, uneasiness, or queasiness
66. Exudate — fluid, such as pus, that leaks out of an infected wound
67. lesion — a pathologic change of the tissues due to disease or injury
68. mucous membrane — Membrane that secretes mucus that lubricates the surface of organs and keeps them moist.
69. myc/o — fungus
70. mycosis — any abnormal condition or disease caused by a fungus
71. neonatology — study of newborns and the treatment of their disorders
72. Palpation — to examine by touch
73. palpitation — pounding or racing heart
74. pyo — pus
75. pyoderma — skin disease containing pus
76. pyel/o — renal pelvis
77. pyelitis — inflammation of the renal pelvis
78. Triage — the medical screening of patients to determine their relative priority of need and the proper place of treatment
79. trauma — wound or injury
80. viral — pertaining to a virus
81. virile — masculine; manly
82. eupnea — normal breathing
83. tension — pressure
84. interstitial — between, but not within, the parts of a tissue
85. Instramuscular — within the muscle
86. Anatomy — The study of body structure
87. Physiology — The study of body function
88. aden/o — gland
89. adip/o — fat
90. anter/o — before, in front
91. midsagittal plane — divides the body into equal right and left sides
92. sagittal plane — divides body into left and right
93. transverse plane — horizontal division of the body into upper and lower portions
94. caud/o — lower part of body, tail
95. Cephal/o — head
96. cyt/o, -cyte (root, suffix) — cell
97. Histology — study of tissues
98. end-, endo- — within, inside
99. Proximal — Closer to the point of attachment, near midline
100. Distal — farther from the origin of a body part or the point of attachment of a limb to the body trunk
101. Medial — Toward the midline of the body
102. Lateral — away from the midline
103. ventral cavity — located along the front of the body, contains the body organs that sustain homeostasis
104. dorsal cavity — includes the cranial and spinal cavities., back part of body
105. vertical plane — an up-and-down plane that is a right angle to the horizon
106. Horizontal Plane (transverse) — divides the body into upper and lower portions. Rotation occurs within the horizontal plane
107. Anterior (ventral) — front of the body
108. Superior — Higher on the body, nearer to the head
109. Inferior — Lowermost, toward the feet
110. Cephalic — toward the head
111. Caudal — toward the tail, lower part of body
112. thoriac cavity — chest
113. Diaphragm — a dome-shaped, muscular partition separating the thorax from the abdomen in mammals. It plays a major role in breathing, as its contraction increases the volume of the thorax and so inflates the lungs.
114. epithelial tissue — Tissue that covers outside of the body and lines organs and cavities.
115. nervous tissue — Tissue that senses stimuli and transmits signals.
116. connective tissue — A body tissue that provides support for the body and connects all of its parts
117. muscular tissue — Contracts and moves the various parts of the body.
118. Homeostasis — A tendency to maintain a balanced or constant internal state; the regulation of any aspect of body chemistry, such as blood glucose, around a particular level (constant control)
119. Inguinal — groin
120. hypochondriac region — to the right and left of the epigastric region
121. epigastric region — located above the stomach
122. umbilical region — Belly button area
123. hypogastric region — located below the stomach (near pelvic)
124. peritoneum — membrane that lines the abdominal cavity
125. parietal peritoneum — the outer layer of the peritoneum that lines the interior of the abdominal wall
126. mesentery — a fused double layer of the parietal peritoneum that attaches parts of the intestine to the interior abdominal wall
127. visceral peritoneum — covers organs
128. retroperitoneal — located behind the peritoneum
129. cell membrane — surrounds and protects the cell
130. Cytoplasm — the portion of the cell outside the nucleus
131. Nucleus — Structure of cell , controls activities and helps cell divide
132. stem cells — unspecialized cells that are able to renew themselves for long periods of time by cell division
133. Gene — a unit of heredity that is transferred from a parent to offspring and is held to determine some characteristic of the offspring.
134. Genetics — The scientific study of heredity
135. geneticist — a specialist in genetics
136. recessive gene — gene that is expressed only in the absence of a dominant gene
137. Genome — Entire set of genes
138. epithelial tissue — A body tissue that covers the surfaces of the body, inside and out
139. muscle tissue — Tissue made of cells capable of contracting.
140. aplasia — lack of development of an organ or tissue
141. hypoplasia — incomplete development of an organ or tissue
142. anaplasia — lack of differentiation
143. carcin/o — cancer
144. malignant — harmful
145. Begnign — harmless
146. Epidermiologist — specialist in the study of the outbreaks of disease
147. endemic — (adj.) native or confined to a particular region or people; characteristic of or prevalent in a field
148. epidemic — A widespread outbreak of an infectious disease.
149. Pandemic — an epidemic that is geographically widespread
150. Eti- — cause
151. epi- — above
152. postmortem — after death
153. -trophy — development
154. angi/o — blood vessel
155. laryng/o — larynx (voice box)
156. waterborne — is floating or moving on water.
157. vector borne — An infection from a bug bite
158. adenocarcinoma — cancerous tumor of glandular tissue
159. iatrogenic illness — unfavorable response due to prescribed medical treatment
160. Thoriacic — chest
161. Huntington's disease — Genetic disorder that causes progressive deterioration of brain cells. caused by a dominant allele. symptoms do not appear until about the age of 30.
162. communicable disease — a disease that is spread from one host to another
163. Hemophilia — a group of hereditary bleeding disorders in which a blood-clotting factor is missing
164. anomaly — deviation from what is normal
165. hypertrophy — increase in cell size
166. exocrine glands — secrete chemical substances into ducts that lead either to other organs or out of the body
167. stasia — to control
168. nosocomial infection — hospital acquired infection
169. Phenylketonuria — a genetic disorder in which an essential digestive enzyme is missing
170. telemetry — A process in which electronic signals are converted into coded, audible signals; these signals can then be transmitted by radio or telephone to a receiver with a decoder at the hospital.
171. droplet transmission — the spread of diseases such as measles, cold, and flu through large respiratory droplets sprayed by coughing or sneezing onto a nearby person or object
172. idiopathic disorder — an illness without known cause
173. adenectomy — surgical removal of a gland
174. cystic fibrosis — A genetic disorder that is present at birth and affects both the respiratory and digestive systems.
175. dense tissue — such as bone and cartilage, form the joints and framework of the body
176. loose connective tissue — nerve cells and blood vessels are surrounded and supported by this
177. liquid connective tissue — blood and lymph, transport nutrients and waste products throughout the body
178. Cytology — study of structure and function of cells
179. Anterior — front of the body
180. Posterior (dorsal) — back of body
